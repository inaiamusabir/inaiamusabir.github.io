Different kinds of Peonies

Discover the diverse world of peonies with our comprehensive guide to different varieties and types. From classic single blooms to extravagant double-petaled flowers, there's a peony to suit every taste and garden style. Have a look into our list below to learn about the unique characteristics and stunning beauty of each peony type.
Herbaceous Peonies: These traditional peonies feature soft, herbaceous stems that die back in winter. They bloom in a wide range of colors and are prized for their exquisite fragrance and lush, full blooms.
Tree Peonies: Known for their woody stems and large, showy flowers, tree peonies are a stunning addition to any garden. They bloom earlier than herbaceous peonies and often feature unique color variations and patterns.
Intersectional Peonies (Itoh Peonies): A cross between herbaceous and tree peonies, intersectional peonies combine the best traits of both types. They produce large, long-lasting blooms on sturdy stems and come in a range of vibrant colors.
Single Bloom Peonies: Characterized by a single layer of petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens, single bloom peonies exude simplicity and elegance. They often have a delicate fragrance and are perfect for adding a touch of charm to bouquets and arrangements.
Double Bloom Peonies: Double bloom peonies boast multiple layers of petals, creating a lush and luxurious appearance. These showy flowers come in a variety of colors and forms, from fully double to semi-double, and are prized for their romantic beauty.
Japanese Peonies: Japanese peonies feature large, cup-shaped blooms with a central cluster of petals surrounded by a contrasting collar of smaller petals. They often have a striking appearance and add a touch of drama to the garden.
Anemone Peonies: Anemone peonies have a unique appearance, with a central cluster of fluffy petals surrounded by a ring of larger, flat petals. They create a striking focal point in the garden and are often sought after for their unusual beauty.
